This is a Yoruba praise poem I wrote for my Africana Studies
professor, and of course for the orisa Mother Oya.
Enjoy!


By Paul Morrison

For Mei Mei Sanford

 

Oriki Ọya

Praise to Mother Ọya, Spirit Of The Wind!

Flying in the clouds, she squeezes them, laughing.

Ọya, the man is looking for you, but he does not find you.

Lightning from the sky, you burn his house,

but as he cries, you send him a cool wind,

and reveal a warm sun.

When the men make the wall, it is made of stone.

How easily she could blow it down!

Mother Ọya!

Powerful Òrìṣà, Knower Of Secrets!

She tells the grandfathers in their graves of the deeds of their grandsons.

The grandsons walk for many miles,

and dip their toes into each finger of Odo-Ọya!

As the water flows, she sees them dance for her.

And when the wind comes, we dance with her.

But then the wind goes,

and she is running to a new place.

Only Ọya knows her destination.

Mother Ọya!

When the woman sees the rainbow and cries out,

she is there, every color.

When the child pokes his eye with a stick and cries out,

she is there, every splinter.

When the man feels the fire in his loins and cries out,

she is there, every drop

of sweat on the woman’s skin.

Mother Ọya!

Blow away the storms in the sky,

and the ones inside my heart!

Take away the little rocks in my shoes,

and the big ones in my eyes!

Wash away the dry and cracked dirt from around the well,

and from my face!

Move away from the cliffs the men who are lost!

Send your strong breath to guide them back to their houses,

for I am among them.

 

Paul A. Morrison
