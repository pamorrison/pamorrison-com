Hello and welcome to my website!

My name is Paul Morrison. I am an undergraduate Senior at The College of William & Mary, and I am studying primarily Mathematics and Computer Science. This site is a collection of things I've created, things I know, and things to know about me, and I built it from scratch with a lot of love. To the left you'll find areas of the site to explore. A short bio

I currently reside in Williamsburg, VA, but I was born and raised in Texas, and I lived there until late 2014.

After I graduated high school, I spent a few years bouncing around jobs. I had a bit of consistency on the job front, as I had a few tech jobs but mostly food industry stuff, like grocery stores and pizza chains. I landed at Pizza Hut when I was 21, where I stayed for a long time.

After working for Pizza Hut for about 10 years, I went into restaurant management and spent close to a year and a half living pretty successfully. However, I burned out on that eventually and ended up quitting pretty unceremoniously. I worked as a waiter for a few different restaurants after that, without much inspiration or direction.

However, I cleaned up my act and moved out to Virginia to start over and give my education a real shot. I started at Thomas Nelson Community College in the spring of 2015 and after 2 1/2 years with a 4.0, I got an Associate's Degree with a specialization in Computer Science. During my time there, I began tutoring and realized that I loved teaching and math, so I decided to focus on those. I applied to only two 4-year schools, Christopher Newport University, and The College of William & Mary, and the latter decided I should get a healthy financial aid package, so I accepted and started there in the Fall of 2017.

Since then I have become really passionate about Computer Science, and my intention is to enter W&M's graduate school to obtain a Master's Degree in Computer Science. I'm still a math major as of now, and I enjoy the subject, but my interests have both broadened and deepened in the last year. In the future, I would like to work for NASA before I go into education, and I was recently hired by SAIC to do exactly that, so that's the plan for the near-term. Thanks for indulging me, and I genuinely hope you're having a great day!
